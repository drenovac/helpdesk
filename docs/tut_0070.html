The first component of pay class calculations is the Award Interpreter, and in the sample setup below we've selected to chop up Monday into the first 2 hours, followed by a slot of 3, followed by a further 2, etc. This is for demonstration purposes only. You will configure your own award as fits your needs.

Here is where we kludge the AI into doing things for us, becuase various configuration pieces are in place.

Please note rows 1-4 are the same VOT codes as in rows 6-9, however with very different rates of pay.
This is to demonstrate on the scheduling workbench, that the elements in rows 1-4 are being pulled through.

If you now go to the scheduling workbench, and open a shift that is paid through the award that is configured above, noting the type of shift is "Normal Day". That's to tell the system to do things as always.

Then on the LHS you will notice a "Pays" dropdown - this is a facility where you the operator can force the system to start the shift at a different pay level.
That pay level (and hence the display) reflects directly on the "Overtime Base Rate" configuration as shown above.

 

In this example, we choose to pay the pay-rate / code "VOT Mon-Fri", and so select it on the dropdown.
Open the costs tab, and note what has transpired, partocularly the hourly rates of pay.
Then note, the breakup of the shift hours is as per the definition of "Overtime Spans" as we mentioned in the detail of the award interpreter (above).

 

The "Pays" function allows the operator to set how the award interpreter is to handle the payment for the shift, whilst maintaining the rules within the confines of the pay class (classification code)
