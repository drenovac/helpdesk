Regarding SA part public holidays:

This year they fall on: 24/12/2013, & 31/12/2013 and apply from 1900 to 0000 on these dates only.

Overview

The requirement is to pay Public Holiday loading on a non-Public Holiday (in this case on Xmas and New Year's Eve), between the hours of 19.00 - 00.00.

Shown below is the completed configuration - explanation details:

  1. 01/12/13 represents the most current version of the classification code (pay class).
  2. 24/12/13 is the first date on which a specific award change has to
    25/12/13 is the next day following the award change, the changes to the award have to come back to the baseline (your original award details)
    31/12/13 is the second occurence where the award changes has to be made to pay PHOL rates for the required period
    01/01/14 is then the next occurence where the award returns to the baseline details


The image shows the Award Rule on 01/07/13 & 01/13/13 as "CSW".

 


The Award Rule for 24/12/13 & 31/12/13 is shown as "CSWSAPHOL". The name can be anything you chose, our name is meant to reflect some relationship to its purpose.

This is the only value that needs to be modified on the classification codes for the 2 dates in question.

The Award Interpreter for the baseline award is shown below, and is quite basic.

The award appears to be a generic span loading (06.00 - 18.00 - 06.00), with shift loading applicable in the 18.00 - 06.00 period.

 

The award change for the 24/12 and 31/12 dates, both of which are "Tuesday" is shown below, with the following detail:

The 06.00 - 18.00 span is changed to 06.00 - 19.00
The span of 19.00 - 23.59:59 is re-configured as Normal Hours + PHL (Public Holiday Loading)
The 00.00 - 06.00 span on Wednesday is also showing Normal Hours + PHL (Public Holiday Loading).

It's important to understand how this works:
This definition even though is shows Sunday - Saturday and Public Holiday only applies to the day of the week "Tuesday". Refer to the initial images above to note that this award Rule applies to these two dates only.

When a shift falls (starts) on the 24th or 31st, this award rule will be used to determine what to pay, and that's why there's a PHL entry in the Wednesday column. If a shift starting on the 24th or 31st runs into the next day, this definition will flow into how the Wednesday looks in this Award Rule. The system will not read a new award rule for Wednesday - it's award rule has been selected and set based on the shift start date.
