A customer is having issues with an employee being picked up in a payrun. The payroll exception report is showing an error of "Different Pay Class Type in : FLAT 1 is possibly not configured".

See Also

Helpdesk Index pre-2013 technotes

Explanation

In the course of payroll processing, various validations and edits are carried out against all employees selected for payment.

In the case of this particular problem, we recommend you debug the process in the following order:

  • Confirm that the employee has shifts in the payroll processing period
  • Open a shift within that period and confirm that the shift is correctly costed
  • Check the employee's payroll rates tab to confirm their payment processing details

 

Confirming the employee's shifts through the scheduling workbench confirms that the employee has shifts for processing and that they are correctly costed.
The secondary validation that you get from checking the costs of the shift is to confirm that the shifts are not actually already paid in another pay-run. When you edit a shift, the system will indicate that the shift is already paid (were that the case).

Verifying the employees pay details, shows the following:


What this shows is that the Pay Class does not have a description, which means that the control record for the pay class does not exist.

If you look carefully, you will see that there's a space in the text, that this classification is in fact "FLAT 1", not "FLAT1", and hence the cause of the error.


This particular diagnosis indicates that this employee's record was changed post the shifts being processed into the system, as they show the correct code of "FLAT1".

And, it shows that the entry was made manually rather than via a selection of a dropdown - which would automatically populate the description field.
