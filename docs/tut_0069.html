The VOT

To correctly understand how VOT is implemented, please refer to this earlier article "Voluntary Overtime Award Configuration".

The implemenation of VOT works on the premise of paying the shift hours on the basis of the OT1, OT2 and OT3 time spans. These time spans, whilst configurable in the award interpreter (for duration) & classification codes window for pay_class - are based on hours of the day, not time-zoned hours of the day.

By 'time-zoned' hours, we mean Afternoon, Night, etc.
It means the first 'n' hours of the shift, the next 'p' hours of the shift and then generally the balance.

Looking at the screen shot below:

The configuration is saying:

  • On the Monday, take the first 2 hours of the shift & pay at what ever code is in row 6 of the pay_classification, then take the next 3 hours and pay those housr according to whatever code is in row 7, etc...
  • There is no consideration given for time of day - only hours of the shift.

 

  • Therefore to achieve what you require, you must select to pay the employee at a different pay_class.
    That payclass has to be configured so that it mimics the normal class to which you are creating the OVT rule. This allows you to select the exact pay_codes you wish to use for the VOT hours.

Scheduling Workbench

Then to engage that particular pay class, you change the "Classification Code" of the shift to the newly create pay classification.
